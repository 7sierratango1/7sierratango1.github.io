Diablo IV DLC Theory: Why an Amazonian Oracle / Shadow Priest Is the Most Plausible New Class

By 7SierraTango1 · Independent Analysis

As Blizzard prepares to expand Diablo IV, one question continues to dominate community discussion: what is the unrevealed class coming with the DLC? With the Paladin already confirmed, attention has shifted entirely to the second, still-unannounced addition.

Based on Diablo lore, Blizzard’s long-standing expansion patterns, and the state of the game through Season 11, one theory emerges as the most internally consistent: an Amazonian Oracle / Shadow Priest, native to Skovos and centered on faith, prophecy, and corruption rather than brute force or sanctioned holy power.

Skovos Is the Anchor

Skovos is not merely a new destination. In established Diablo lore, it is inseparable from Amazon civilization—an ancient, insular culture defined not only by martial discipline, but also by spiritual authority, prophecy, and ritual leadership.

Blizzard rarely introduces a major region without tying it to a playable identity. When a location carries this much cultural and spiritual significance, a class that merely passes through it feels unlikely.

The Paladin Sets the Counterpoint

The Paladin’s inclusion is revealing. As a holy warrior aligned with institutional belief, the Paladin represents order, doctrine, and sanctioned faith. Blizzard expansions rarely present a single ideological viewpoint.

If one class embodies structured belief, the narrative naturally invites a counterweight—one that explores belief outside official doctrine.

Why Shadow, Not Necromancy

Shadow in Diablo is not necromancy. It represents doubt, despair, fear, and the erosion of certainty.

Mephisto’s influence thrives in this domain, corrupting loyalty, conviction, and belief itself.

>Why Other Classes Are Unlikely

Monk

The Monk archetype is rooted in eastern philosophy and balance. Skovos is defined by Amazonian prophecy and matriarchal spiritual authority. Introducing a Monk would import an external identity rather than elevate a region-native one.

Crusader

The Crusader occupies the same ideological and mechanical space as the Paladin. With the Paladin already revealed, adding a Crusader would create redundancy instead of contrast.

Witch Doctor

Witch Doctors align with jungle mysticism and spirit pacts, overlapping heavily with Necromancer and Druid themes and tying more closely to Nahantu than Skovos.

Assassin

The Assassin excels at covert precision but lacks the narrative scale required for an expansion focused on Hatred, belief, and ideological corruption.

Pure Amazon (Warrior)

A traditional Amazon warrior fits Skovos geographically but lacks the spiritual dimension necessary to anchor the expansion’s core themes.

The Most Plausible Outcome

If the DLC aims to deepen Diablo IV’s exploration of belief, power, and Hatred, then an Amazonian Oracle / Shadow Priest stands out as the most plausible choice.

This article is speculative and based on publicly available lore and observed design trends. Blizzard has not confirmed the unrevealed class.
